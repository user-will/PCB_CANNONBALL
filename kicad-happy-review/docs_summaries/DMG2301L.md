# DMG2301L — Application Summary

**Manufacturer:** Diodes Incorporated
**Type:** P-Channel Enhancement-Mode MOSFET (−20 V, −3 A, logic-level)
**Package:** SOT23 (3-lead)
**Datasheet:** Copyright © 2021 Diodes Incorporated (`docs/components/DMG2301L/DMG2301L.md`)

> This summary is intended as a reference for validating a board design that uses the DMG2301L. It covers electrical, application-circuit, and layout considerations only. Packaging, mechanical, soldering, and marking information is intentionally omitted.

---

## 1. Short Description

The DMG2301L is a **P-channel enhancement-mode MOSFET** optimized for low on-state resistance while retaining fast switching, targeting **high-efficiency power-management** functions. As a P-channel device it conducts when the gate is pulled **negative** relative to the source (V_GS < V_GS(TH)) and turns off when gate and source are at the same potential.

Typical roles in a design:

- **High-side load switch / reverse-polarity & reverse-current blocking** — source to the rail, drain to the load, gate pulled low to turn on. Its low R_DS(ON) and logic-level threshold make it well suited to driving directly from a GPIO or a small pull-down.
- **Power-path / supply ORing and inrush control.**
- **DC-DC converters, backlighting, motor-control drive stages** (per the datasheet's stated applications).

Key traits relevant to application:

- **Logic-level gate:** full enhancement specified at V_GS = −4.5 V; usable (higher R_DS(ON)) at V_GS = −2.5 V.
- **Low input capacitance** (C_iss ≈ 476 pF) and **low gate charge** (Q_g ≈ 5.5 nC) → fast switching, easy to drive.
- **Integrated body diode** (drain→source), forward-conducting when the device is reverse-biased.

Because the minimum magnitude is only 0.4 V, ensure the gate is held within a few hundred mV of the source when the device must stay **off** — stray coupling or slow gate pull-up can partially enhance it. For reliable **on** state, drive |V_GS| ≥ 4.5 V where possible to hit the 120 mΩ spec.

### 2.4 Characteristic Curves (relevant to electrical sizing)

- **Junction capacitance vs. V_DS (Fig. 10):** C_iss is fairly flat (~450–500 pF above a few volts); C_oss and C_rss fall steeply from ~400 pF / ~250 pF near 0 V to ~40–50 pF by 20 V. Drive-strength and dV/dt estimates should use the capacitance at the operating V_DS, not the headline figures.
- **Transient thermal impedance (Fig. 13):** Z_θJA(t) = r(t) × R_θJA with R_θJA = 142 °C/W; single-pulse r(t) drops well below 0.1 for pulses < ~1 ms, allowing much higher instantaneous dissipation than the 1.5 W steady-state rating. Use for pulsed-load / inrush sizing.
- **Safe Operating Area (Fig. 12):** bounded by R_DS(ON) at low V_DS and by T_J(max) = 150 °C; characterized at T_A = 25 °C, V_GS = 4.5 V, single pulse. Keep the operating (V_DS, I_D) point inside the SOA for the relevant pulse width.

## 3. Sample Applications & Reference Circuit

The datasheet does not provide a dedicated application schematic; the following are the standard configurations implied by the device type and its specs.

### 3.1 High-Side Load Switch (most common use)

```
        VRAIL ──┬─────────── S ┐
                │              │ DMG2301L (P-ch)
               (load rail)     │   D ──────── LOAD ── GND
                               │
          Rpull-up (S→G) ──────┤ G
                               │
   Enable ── Rg ── [NMOS/GPIO] ┘  (pulls G low → turns P-ch ON)
```

- **Source on the supply side, drain on the load.** Gate idles at the source potential (device OFF) via a **gate pull-up resistor (G→S)**; pulling the gate low (through a series gate resistor + small NMOS or open-drain GPIO) enhances the device ON.
- **Gate pull-up** (e.g. 100 kΩ): guarantees OFF default and bounds V_GS to the rail; on rails > 8 V add a **resistor divider or Zener clamp** so V_GS stays within ±8 V.
- **Series gate resistor (R_g):** slows the gate edge to control inrush dV/dt and limit ringing/EMI; trades against switching loss. With Q_g ≈ 5.5 nC the gate is easy to drive — R_g is mainly for slew control.
- **Body-diode orientation:** the intrinsic diode conducts drain→source. In a high-side switch (source=input) the body diode points from load back to source, so the switch does **not** block reverse current when off — add a back-to-back FET or a series diode if reverse blocking is required.

### 3.2 Reverse-Battery / Reverse-Current Protection

A P-channel high-side FET with source at the battery and gate referenced through a pull-down/Zener is a classic low-loss reverse-polarity block; conduction loss ≈ I² × R_DS(ON) (120 mΩ) instead of a diode drop. Confirm V_GS stays within ±8 V across the battery range.

### 3.3 Switching / DC-DC Use

Low Q_g and sub-30 ns switching support PWM use up to moderate frequencies. Sizing must respect P_D = 1.5 W (Note-5 board) and the SOA; switching loss ≈ ½·V·I·(t_R+t_F)·f_sw plus gate-drive loss Q_g·V_GS·f_sw.

## 4. Component Placement, Routing & Layout Best Practices

The datasheet gives no explicit layout section beyond the suggested pad layout; the following combines its thermal basis with standard MOSFET practice.

- **Copper area = heatsink:** the 83 °C/W rating **requires** a 1"×1", 2 oz copper pour. With minimal pads R_θJA rises to ~142 °C/W. Maximize copper on the drain (and source) pads and use the largest practical pour for the dissipated power; thermal performance is dominated by board copper, not the SOT23 itself.
- **Size drain/source traces for full current:** at up to −3 A, widen the power traces to limit IR drop and self-heating; thin traces add series resistance on top of R_DS(ON).
- **Keep the gate loop short:** route the gate trace and its pull-up/series-R close to the device to minimize loop inductance and avoid spurious turn-on/ringing from fast dV/dt at the drain. Place R_g adjacent to the gate pin.
- **Gate protection placement:** any V_GS clamp (Zener) or divider must sit close to the gate–source pins to be effective against transients, given the ±8 V V_GSS limit.
- **Switching-node containment:** keep the high-dV/dt drain node compact to limit capacitive coupling/EMI; avoid running sensitive signals beneath or alongside it.
- **Thermal vias / pour stitching:** if a ground or rail pour is used as the heat-spreader, stitch it well; transient loads can be handled per the Fig. 13 Z_θJA curve, but steady dissipation must keep T_J = T_A + P_D·R_θJA below +150 °C with margin.

## 6. Design Validation Checklist

- [ ] V_DS never exceeds −20 V (incl. transients/ringing); derate for margin.
- [ ] **V_GS kept within ±8 V** under all drive conditions — clamp/divide on rails ≥ 8 V.
- [ ] Gate driven to |V_GS| ≥ 4.5 V for the 120 mΩ on-state; if only −2.5 V is available, design around 150 mΩ.
- [ ] Gate has a defined OFF state (pull-up to source for P-ch high-side) — not left floating; accounts for the −0.4 V worst-case threshold.
- [ ] Continuous I_D within rating **for the actual board copper** (−3 A only on the Note-5 board; ~−1 A on minimal copper).
- [ ] Operating (V_DS, I_D) point inside SOA for the relevant pulse width (Fig. 12).
- [ ] Thermal check: T_J = T_A + P_D·R_θJA < +150 °C with margin, using the R_θJA that matches actual copper area (83 vs 142 °C/W).
- [ ] Reverse-current path through the body diode considered (add back-to-back FET / series diode if blocking needed).
- [ ] Gate series resistor sized for desired inrush/switching dV/dt vs. switching loss.
- [ ] SOT23 pad assignment (G/S/D = pins 1/2/3) matches the footprint.

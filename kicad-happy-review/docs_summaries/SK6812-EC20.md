# SK6812-EC20 — Application Summary

> Intelligent (addressable) RGB LED with integrated control IC, 2.0 × 2.0 × 0.65 mm "2020" package.
> Source: *SK6812-EC20 LED Datasheet* (Rev. 04, 2019-05-15) and supporting figures.
> Purpose of this document: reference for validating a board design that uses the SK6812-EC20.

---

## 1. Short Description

The SK6812-EC20 (a.k.a. **SK6812-2020**) is a **smart, single-wire addressable RGB LED**: it integrates an RGB die and a serial-cascade constant-current control IC in one 2020-size SMD package. Each device is one independently-controllable pixel.

Key functional traits:

- **Single-wire serial protocol** (unipolar RZ / return-to-zero), 24-bit color per pixel (8-bit G, 8-bit R, 8-bit B → 256 levels each, transmitted **G7…G0, R7…R0, B7…B0**, MSB first).
- **Cascadable**: data shifts in on `DIN`, one pixel's worth of data is latched, the remainder is reshaped and forwarded on `DOUT` to the next pixel.
- **Built-in signal-reshaping amplifier** → regenerates the waveform at every pixel, so cascade length is limited by transmission speed rather than signal degradation.
- **Built-in constant-current drivers** (per-color), high-precision internal RC oscillator, power-on-reset.
- **PWM dimming** of the LED output (patented PWM, ~1.2 kHz refresh of the LED).
- Low drive voltage, designed for the same family/protocol as WS2812-class devices.

**Pixel role on a board:** typically arranged in long chains (strips, matrices, point lights). Only the first pixel connects to the MCU; all others are fed by the previous pixel's `DOUT`.

| 1 | **VDD**  | Power supply (+) |
| 2 | **DOUT** | Serial data output to next pixel |

Pad layout (top view): VDD bottom-right, DOUT top-right, GND top-left, DIN bottom-left. Pads ~0.8 × 0.7 mm, 0.5 mm gaps (see §5).

### 3.3 Optical / Drive Current (per color die)

Two current binnings exist; **"EC20-6812 = 12 mA" variant** is the relevant one for SK6812-EC20:

| Color | Dominant λ (nm) | Luminance @12 mA (mcd) | Luminous flux @12 mA (lm) |
|-------|-----------------|------------------------|---------------------------|
| Red   | 620–630 | 300–500 | 0.8–2.0 |
| Green | 520–535 | 400–700 | 2.0–3.5 |
| Blue  | 460–475 | 100–300 | 0.5–1.5 |

- **Per-color drive current ≈ 12 mA** (constant-current, set by the IC). Worst-case ("white", all three on) draws roughly **3 × 12 mA ≈ 36 mA per pixel** plus ~1 mA quiescent.
- Use this for **power-budget and copper sizing**: e.g. a 100-pixel chain at full white ≈ 100 × ~37 mA ≈ **3.7 A @ 5 V (~18 W)**.
- Viewing/radiation angle ≈ **160°** (wide, Lambertian-like).

### 3.4 Timing / Switching (VCC = 5 V, Ta = 25 °C)

| Parameter | Symbol | Min | Typ | Max | Unit | Conditions |
|-----------|--------|-----|-----|-----|------|------------|
| Data rate | f<sub>DIN</sub> | — | 800 | — | kHz | 67% duty for "1" |
| DIN→DOUT propagation delay | T<sub>PLH</sub>/T<sub>PHL</sub> | — | — | 500 | ns | |
| Output rise/fall time | Tr/Tf | — | 100 | — | ns | |

**Bit-encoding (RZ, code period T = 1.2 µs):**

| Symbol | Meaning | Min | Std | Max | Unit |
|--------|---------|-----|-----|-----|------|
| T0H | "0" high time | 0.2 | 0.32 | 0.4 | µs |
| T0L | "0" low time  | — | 0.8 | — | µs |
| T1H | "1" high time | 0.58 | 0.64 | 1.0 | µs |
| T1L | "1" low time  | — | 0.2 | — | µs |
| Trst | Reset (latch) low time | > 80 | — | — | µs |

- Every bit starts HIGH; the HIGH width selects 0 vs 1. The LOW portion of any bit must be **< 20 µs** (else interpreted as reset).
- **Reset/latch:** hold `DIN` LOW for **≥ 80 µs** to latch the frame and display it.
- At 800 kbps, 24 bits/pixel → ~30 µs/pixel; max chain length for a target frame rate is bandwidth-limited (e.g. ~30 fps for large arrays).

## 4. Sample Application & Reference Circuit

**Typical cascade topology** (from datasheet §16):

```
                R1 ~500Ω
Controller DOUT ──/\/\── DIN  U1 DOUT ──────► DIN  U2 DOUT ──── … ──► DIN  Un  DOUT (last, open or test pad)
Controller VSS  ─────────── GND (common)
        5V ──────┬───────────┬───────────┬─────  (shared rail)
              C(100nF)    C(100nF)    C(100nF)
                 │           │           │
                GND         GND         GND
```

Reference-circuit details:

- **Series data resistor R1 ≈ 500 Ω** placed close to the **controller's data output**, in series with the first pixel's `DIN`. Damps reflections/ringing and limits surge/ESD current into the data pin.
  - For **short** runs (e.g. on-board strips), ~500 Ω at the source is recommended.
  - For **long** runs / modules, the value is adjusted empirically per cable type/length (datasheet notes the exact value is "fixed in actual use").
- **Decoupling capacitor per pixel:** one **100 nF (marked "104")** ceramic from `VDD` to `GND`, as close to the pixel as possible. The datasheet explicitly calls the inter-bead capacitor "essential" for stable operation.
- **Common ground** between the controller and the LED chain is mandatory (single-wire protocol is ground-referenced).
- **Cascade rule:** `DOUT(n) → DIN(n+1)`. Only the first pixel sees the controller; each pixel reshapes and forwards the remaining data.
- **Data direction is fixed** — observe `DIN`/`DOUT` orientation in layout; reversing breaks the chain.

## 5. Footprint, Placement, Routing & Layout Best Practices

### 5.1 Recommended PCB land pattern (from §6, top view)

- 4 rectangular pads, each ≈ **0.8 mm (outer) × 0.7 mm (height)** — geometry: pad width 0.8 mm, center gap 0.5 mm (horizontal), 0.5 mm vertical gap between the two pad rows.
- Pad assignment matches §2 (GND top-left, DOUT top-right, DIN bottom-left, VDD bottom-right).
- Body 2.0 × 2.0 mm, height 0.65 mm; tolerance ±0.1 mm.

### 5.2 Power distribution

- **Size the VDD/GND rails for full-white current** (~37 mA/pixel). Long chains develop significant **IR drop** along the 5 V rail → inject power at both ends or at intervals to keep VDD ≥ 3.7 V at the far pixels and to avoid color shift.
- Place a **local 100 nF decoupling cap at every pixel** (per reference circuit). Add bulk electrolytic/ceramic capacitance (e.g. several hundred µF to low-mF) at each power-injection point to absorb the large transient when many pixels switch simultaneously.
- Keep `GND` low-impedance and continuous; the data signal is referenced to it.

### 5.3 Data routing

- Keep the **`DOUT→DIN` hop between adjacent pixels short**; rely on the per-pixel reshaping for long chains rather than long single nets.
- Put the **~500 Ω series resistor at the driving end** of each data link (controller output, and optionally at long inter-module jumps) to control ringing and protect the input.
- For inter-board/long cable runs, keep data and ground paired; consider termination tuning per §16.
- Respect input thresholds when driving the first pixel from low-voltage logic (see §3.2 note — level shifting for 3.3 V controllers).

### 5.4 Thermal / derating (from §17 performance graph)

- **Light output drops with thermal-pad temperature**, and **red derates fastest**:
  - Blue/Green: ~100% → ~80% as pad temp rises 20 °C → 120 °C.
  - Red: ~100% → ~54% over the same range (steeper) — expect **color shift toward blue/green as the board heats up**.
- Manufacturer guidance: keep **operating (pin) temperature ≤ 60 °C**. Provide adequate copper/thermal relief, avoid dense clustering without heat-spreading, and choose drive duty / ambient accordingly.
- Account for self-heating in dense arrays when setting maximum sustained brightness.

### 5.5 Handling / robustness (electrical-relevant only)

- **ESD-sensitive (2 kV HBM):** protect exposed data lines at connectors; series resistor at the data input also mitigates surge.
- Eliminate possibility of surge/EOS on the data and power lines during design.

## 6. Quick Design-Validation Checklist

- [ ] VDD within **3.7–5.5 V** at every pixel (check far-end IR drop, target ~5 V).
- [ ] **100 nF decoupling cap per pixel**, placed adjacent to VDD/GND pads.
- [ ] **~500 Ω series resistor** on the data line at the controller output.
- [ ] **Common ground** between controller and LED chain.
- [ ] First-pixel `DIN` driven at **V<sub>IH</sub> ≥ 0.7·VDD** (level shift if MCU is 3.3 V and VDD = 5 V).
- [ ] `DOUT(n) → DIN(n+1)` chain orientation correct; data direction consistent.
- [ ] Power rails sized for **~37 mA/pixel** worst case; power injection / bulk caps for long chains.
- [ ] Footprint matches recommended land pattern; thermal design keeps pin temp **≤ 60 °C**.
- [ ] Reset/latch low time **≥ 80 µs** honored by firmware; bit timing within T0H/T1H windows.

# AP2112 — Application Summary

**Manufacturer:** Diodes Incorporated
**Type:** 600 mA CMOS Low-Dropout (LDO) Linear Regulator with Enable
**Datasheet:** June 2017 revision (`docs/components/AP2112/AP2112.md`)

> This summary is intended as a reference for validating a board design that uses the AP2112. It covers electrical, application-circuit, and layout considerations only. Packaging, mechanical, soldering, and marking information is intentionally omitted.

---

## 1. Short Description

The AP2112 is a CMOS-process low-dropout linear voltage regulator that delivers a guaranteed **600 mA (min.)** continuous output current at a fixed output voltage. It is offered in several factory-trimmed output options (**1.2 V, 1.8 V, 2.5 V, 2.6 V, 3.3 V**) with **±1.5 % output accuracy**.

Key functional traits relevant to the application:

- **Enable (EN) pin** for ON/OFF control of the output; internal **3 MΩ pull-down** holds the device off when EN is left floating.
- **Auto-discharge**: when disabled, the output is pulled to ground through an internal **60 Ω** resistor.
- **Foldback short-circuit protection** (limits short current to ~50 mA), **over-temperature shutdown (OTSD)**, and fast loop response for good line/load transient behavior.
- **Low quiescent current** (55 µA typ.) and **very low standby current** (0.01 µA typ.), making it suitable for battery-powered and portable equipment.
- Stable with a **1.0 µF** output capacitor over ceramic, tantalum, and aluminum electrolytic types.

Typical applications: laptop/portable computers, LCD monitors, portable consumer electronics, and general point-of-load regulation from a higher rail or battery.

### 2.4 Dropout Voltage (V_DROP)

Dropout is strongly dependent on the selected output option:

| Output Option | I_OUT = 10 mA (typ/max) | I_OUT = 300 mA (typ/max) | I_OUT = 600 mA (typ/max) | Unit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2.5 V / 2.6 V / 3.3 V | 5 / 8 | 125 / 200 | 250 / 400 | mV |
| 1.2 V | — / 1300 | — / 1300 | — / 1300 | mV |
| 1.8 V | — / 700 | — / 700 | — / 700 | mV |

> For the common 3.3 V option, the dropout is **250 mV typ. (400 mV max)** at 600 mA. Allow this headroom when sizing the upstream rail (e.g. 3.3 V output needs ≥ ~3.7 V in at full load worst-case).

### 2.5 Enable (EN) Pin Logic

| Symbol | Parameter | Min | Max | Unit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| V_IH | EN High (regulator ON) | 1.5 | 6.0 | V |
| V_IL | EN Low (regulator OFF) | 0 | 0.4 | V |
| R_PD | Internal EN pull-down | — | 3.0 (typ) | MΩ |

- EN is **CMOS-logic compatible** down to 1.5 V high threshold — can be driven directly by most MCU GPIOs and 1.8 V logic.
- The internal 3 MΩ pull-down guarantees the device defaults **OFF** when EN is unconnected/floating. Tie EN to V_IN (directly or via a pull-up) for "always-on" operation.
- EN must not be left floating if deterministic startup is required.

### 2.6 Auto-Discharge

When EN is taken low, the output node is actively discharged to GND through an internal **60 Ω (R_DCHG)** resistor — useful for ensuring a known-low output during power sequencing.

### 2.7 Thermal Resistance

| Package | θ_JA (junction–ambient, no heatsink) | θ_JC (junction–case) | Unit |
|---|---|---|---|
| SOT25 (SOT-23-5) | 184 | 96 | °C/W |
| SO-8 (SOIC-8) | 114 | 75 | °C/W |
| SOT89-5 | 120 | 47 | °C/W |

**Power-dissipation / thermal sizing:** As a linear regulator, P_D ≈ (V_IN − V_OUT) × I_OUT + V_IN × I_Q. Junction temperature T_J = T_A + P_D × θ_JA must stay below +125 °C (operating) with margin. The SOT25 package has the highest θ_JA (184 °C/W) and is the most thermally constrained — at high V_IN−V_OUT differentials and high load, verify T_J or choose SO-8/SOT89-5 and/or copper-pour heatsinking.

## 3. Sample Applications & Reference Circuit

### 3.1 Typical Application Circuit

The datasheet reference circuit is minimal — input cap, output cap, and an enable control:

```
        VIN ──┬──────────┤ VIN    VOUT ├──────┬──── VOUT (1.2/1.8/2.5/2.6/3.3 V)
              │          │   AP2112    │      │
            CIN 1µF      │             │    COUT 1µF
              │          │             │      │
             GND      ON─┤ EN          │     GND
                     OFF │         GND ├──┐
                         └─────────────┘  │
                                         GND
```

- **C_IN = 1.0 µF**, **C_OUT = 1.0 µF** (datasheet "Typical Applications Circuit").
- **Note 4:** use **X7R or X5R** dielectric when a 1.0 µF ceramic is chosen for input/output capacitors (X5R/X7R maintain capacitance over voltage and temperature; Y5V should be avoided).
- The device is stable with ceramic, tantalum, or aluminum electrolytic output capacitors of 1.0 µF — ceramic is the usual choice for size and ESR.
- **EN control:** drive from a GPIO/logic, or tie to V_IN for always-on. Default state is OFF (3 MΩ internal pull-down).

### 3.2 Functional Behavior Worth Noting in Application

- **Foldback current limit:** the short-circuit limit folds back to ~50 mA. This protects the device but has a startup implication (see below).
- **Fast loop / transient response:** suited for loads with moderate step changes; the 1 µF output cap is sufficient for the rated specs.
- **Start-up time:** ~20 µs (no load) — fast enough for most sequencing needs.

### 3.3 Application Note — Pre-Existing Output Load and Foldback (Important)

From the datasheet Application Note (page 10):

> In unusual applications where a **current load is already present at the output before the part is enabled**, the foldback current limiting may **prevent the part from starting**. This can occur with multiple-supply or negative-supply systems, or in production test where a current source is applied before the device is enabled.

**Design implication:** Avoid configurations where another supply, a back-feeding load, or a test current sources current into V_OUT before EN goes high. With a normal (passive) load, foldback does not interfere with startup. If the AP2112 output can be back-driven (e.g. ORing with another rail, large output bulk capacitance plus a pre-biased load), evaluate startup behavior explicitly.

## 4. Component Placement, Routing & Layout Best Practices

The datasheet provides limited explicit layout guidance for this part, so the following combines its stated recommendations with standard LDO layout practice:

- **Input capacitor (C_IN, 1 µF):** place as close as possible to the V_IN pin, with the return tied directly to the device GND. Minimizes input-line inductance and supply ringing, especially important with the foldback/transient behavior.
- **Output capacitor (C_OUT, 1 µF):** place close to the V_OUT pin with a short, direct return to GND. Required for loop stability — do not omit or undersize below the 1.0 µF specified value.
- **Capacitor dielectric:** use **X7R or X5R** ceramics (per Note 4) so effective capacitance is retained across DC bias and temperature. Avoid Y5V/Z5U.
- **Ground:** provide a low-impedance ground connection from the GND pin to the system ground plane; keep the C_IN and C_OUT return currents on a clean, short path. For SO-8, both pins 6 and 7 are GND — tie both.
- **Thermal:** for higher dissipation, widen V_IN/V_OUT copper and add ground/copper pour around the package to lower effective θ_JA. SOT25's 184 °C/W θ_JA makes copper area meaningful at elevated load and input-output differential.
- **Trace width:** size V_IN and V_OUT traces for the full 600 mA rating to limit IR drop (which directly eats into dropout headroom) and self-heating.
- **EN routing:** route the EN control trace away from noisy/switching nodes; the high-impedance 3 MΩ pull-down means EN can pick up noise — keep the trace short or add a small pull resistor/cap if the driving source is far or noisy. Do not leave EN floating where defined startup is needed.
- **High-voltage spacing:** the datasheet notes that for high-voltage applications, appropriate creepage/clearance distances between terminals and PCB tracking should be observed per industry guidelines (generally not a concern at ≤6 V supply, but noted for completeness).

## 6. Design Validation Checklist

- [ ] V_IN within 2.5–6.0 V under all conditions; never exceeds 6.5 V absolute max (check transients/inrush).
- [ ] V_IN − V_OUT headroom ≥ worst-case dropout at expected I_OUT (e.g. ≥ ~0.4 V at 600 mA for the 3.3 V option).
- [ ] Output current demand (incl. transients) ≤ 600 mA.
- [ ] C_IN = 1 µF and C_OUT = 1 µF (X7R/X5R) present and placed close to pins.
- [ ] EN pin driven to a valid level (V_IH ≥ 1.5 V to enable, V_IL ≤ 0.4 V to disable); not left floating where defined behavior is required.
- [ ] No external source/load back-feeding V_OUT before EN is asserted (foldback startup hazard).
- [ ] Thermal check: T_J = T_A + P_D × θ_JA < +125 °C with margin; copper area adequate for chosen package.
- [ ] Selected output-voltage variant (1.2/1.8/2.5/2.6/3.3 V) matches the ordering code.
- [ ] Pinout matches the exact package/variant footprint (note SOT89-5 R5 vs R5A difference).
